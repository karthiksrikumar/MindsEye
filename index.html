Chapter I  ·  Our Story
A tradition of creative expression

Where Art and
Words Meet

Mind's Eye is a student-run literary magazine at South Windsor High School, publishing original student artwork and creative writing every school year. For over twenty years the magazine has served as a home for the creative voices that fill the hallways of SWHS, from poets and essayists to visual artists and storytellers of every kind.

"Mind's Eye has served as a way for me to artistically express myself in a community that fosters growth and friendship around the arts."
Kyle Garneau, former member

Each year the club collects and reviews student submissions, and then the entire membership votes together on which works will be featured. No single voice decides who is heard. The magazine welcomes fiction, poetry, personal essays, visual art, and every form of creative expression in between.

01

The Magazine

Every spring we release a printed literary magazine featuring original student art and writing, selected and curated by the full editorial board together.

02

Coffee Houses

Throughout the year we host open-mic style coffee house events where students share poetry, music, essays, and art in a warm and welcoming space.

03

Writing Contests

We hold writing and poetry contests open to all SWHS students, with standout work submitted to the Scholastic Art and Writing Awards and beyond.

"It was really fun. People put up decorations, rearranged the furniture, and had a signup sheet for people who wanted to share. There was a lot of poetry, art, and essays."
Sharon Madda, club member, on the coffee house

The coffee house is a beloved tradition: part open-mic night, part community gathering. Coffee and refreshments are served, students sign up to share whatever moves them, and the room is always full of warmth. Musicians, poets, and visual artists all find their audience here. Everyone is welcome, whether or not they are a club member.

Chapter II  ·  The Advisor

Mrs. Amanda Flachsbart, Lead Advisor, Mind's Eye Literary Magazine

Lead Advisor, Mind's Eye

Mrs. Amanda
Flachsbart

Mrs. Flachsbart has been overseeing Mind's Eye for six years, bringing with her a deep love of art and writing and a genuine enthusiasm for the creative work students produce outside the curriculum. Under her guidance the magazine has continued a tradition stretching back more than two decades.

"I love art and writing, and because I follow a specific curriculum I don't always get to see my students' creative writing. I think it'll be a great opportunity for students throughout the building to express themselves creatively."

The club's democratic approach to judging reflects her values: every submission is reviewed by the full membership, ensuring that no single perspective gatekeeps the creative voices of the school.

"The judging itself takes place with all the members and everybody kind of looks and reviews each submission, so that it's not just one or two students deciding on who wins," Flachsbart explained.

Find Your Place
in the Pages

Join the Club

Mind's Eye welcomes any student who loves reading, writing, art, or simply being part of a creative community. The club is relaxed, encouraging, and always open to new voices and new ideas.

"Any student who enjoys reading and/or writing will probably appreciate the club environment. It is very relaxed and encouraging of creativity and freedom to express oneself. We are always open to new people and new ideas."

  • Meetings held in Room 129
  • Ask Mrs. Flachsbart to join the classroom and sign up for Remind notifications
  • No prior experience required, only enthusiasm and an open mind
  • Open to all grades and all backgrounds

Submit Your Work

The magazine accepts student submissions throughout the year. Poetry, fiction, personal essays, and visual art are all welcome. There are no wrong answers when it comes to creative expression.

Each fall we run a writing contest, and each spring an art contest. Submissions are reviewed collectively by all editors, giving every piece a fair and thoughtful read.

  • Writing contest deadline: December
  • Spring art contest announced at the start of each semester
  • Open to all SWHS students, not only club members
  • Fiction, poetry, personal essays, and visual art all accepted
